
One needs to add to three places the signal one wants to see

If possible Chose a non-common signal name that wont match a lot in the
gargantuan log file, for this example we call it "r_w_n"

in the setattr line of "yo_xxx.txt"
in the expose line of "yo_xxx.txt"

and in the set_io line of "oricatmos.pcf" for the desired output pin

At this point it is not certain but it seems yosys & pals always
converts your vhdl signal names to lower casse

./checkout_and_build 2>&1 | tee /tmp/some_temp_file.txt

grep r_w_n /tmp/some_temp_file.txt

.. something like ..


yosys> setattr -set keep 1 \oricatmostop_ice40/w:oric.inst_cpu.r_w_n
yosys> expose \oricatmostop_ice40/w:oric.inst_cpu.r_w_n
New module port: oricatmostop_ice40/oric.inst_cpu.r_w_n
output [0:0] oric.inst_cpu.r_w_n
Info: constrained 'oric.inst_cpu.r_w_n' to bel 'X0/Y21/io0'

Especially check that the .pcf entry is "constrained"
